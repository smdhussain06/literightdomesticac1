Natural Light

Generated naturally, primarily by the Sun.

Physics

Light Interactions

  • Reflection: Ability of surfaces to bounce light (Reflectance).
  • Absorption: Ability to neither reflect nor transmit light.
  • Transmission: Ability of materials to allow light passage (Transmittance).

Philosophy

Lighting Qualities

Ambient Lighting: General illumination throughout an area.

Focal Glow: The pool of light that draws attention and separates the important.

Play of Brilliants: Dynamic light that creates interest and stimulates the spirit.

Drafting

Installation Logic

Drawing in cross-section is essential to communicate how fixtures are integrated into the architecture.

  • Recessed: Embedded in the ceiling/slab for a seamless look.
  • Superimposed: Surface mounted for easy access and retrofitting.
  • Suspended: Hanging fixtures for volume and decorative impact.
03
LiteRight Academy

The Beam of Light

Visualizing the photometric footprint of the luminaire.

  • Narrow Beams: Dramatic focal glow, high contrast.
  • Wide Beams: Uniform ambient luminescence, broad coverage.
  • Directionality: Indicating the tilt and aim to assist on-site installation.
