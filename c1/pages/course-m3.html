Module 3

Spatial Analysis — Reading a Space

Duration: 45 minutes  |  Objective: Systematically analyze any space and its users to establish lighting requirements.

Lesson 3.1 — Identifying Needs of Space & User (20 mins)

The Space Audit Checklist

SPACE PROFILE
├── Dimensions (length × width × height, total m²)
├── Openings (windows, doors, skylights)
│   ├── Orientation (N/S/E/W)
│   ├── Size and height
│   └── Existing treatments (curtains, blinds)
├── Existing light sources
│   ├── Natural light patterns throughout the day
│   └── Artificial fixtures currently installed
├── Architectural features
│   ├── Ceiling type (flat, vaulted, beams)
│   ├── Wall treatments and textures
│   └── Notable details (arches, columns, niches)
└── Furniture layout and activity zones

Lesson 3.2 — Materials and Light Interactions (15 mins)

MaterialBehaviorDesign Implication
Matte black laminateAbsorbs most light; reflects at direct anglesNeeds dedicated task light
White blackout curtainsBlocks exterior, reflects interior lightTurns window into reflective surface
Dark vinyl paintLow reflectance during dayCloudy days need ceiling light compensation
Chukum plasterTextured, enhanced by directional lightUse wall-washing; avoid flat overhead

Lesson 3.3 — Natural Light Analysis (10 mins)

  • Solar path — how sunlight moves across your space
  • Window orientation — North: diffuse / South: warm, direct / East: morning / West: glare
  • Skylights — dramatic zenith source, angle changes seasonally
  • Never design artificial lighting in isolation — start with natural light
