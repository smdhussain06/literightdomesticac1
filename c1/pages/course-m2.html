Module 2

Understanding Light & Perception

Duration: 45 minutes  |  Objective: Grasp the physics of light, human perception, and fundamental metrics.

Lesson 2.1 — What Is Light? (15 mins)

Light is electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye (380–780 nm).

Four Behaviors of Light on Surfaces

BehaviorDescriptionExample
ReflectionLight bounces off a surfaceMirror, polished marble
AbsorptionSurface absorbs light energyDark matte walls
TransmissionLight passes throughClear glass
RefractionLight bends between materialsPrism, water surface
💡 Key Insight: The same light source can produce completely different effects depending on the material it hits. Light design is inseparable from material design.

Lesson 2.2 — The Observer (15 mins)

  • Rods — sensitive to brightness, active in low-light (scotopic vision)
  • Cones — sensitive to color, active in well-lit conditions (photopic vision)
  • Mesopic vision — the transition zone (dusk, dimmed interiors)

Three Types of Visual Adaptation

  1. Light adaptation — dark to bright (~5 seconds)
  2. Dark adaptation — bright to dark (up to 30 minutes)
  3. Chromatic adaptation — adjusting to different color temperatures
