SCENE 1Architectural vs Decorative0:30–3:00
[VISUAL: Split-screen examples]

Architectural — invisible fixtures, light is the point. Efficiency, beam control, minimal presence. Decorative — visible fixtures, part of the design statement. Aesthetic alignment, scale, character. Most spaces need both.

SCENE 2Color Consistency3:00–5:00
[VISUAL: Matched vs mismatched temps]

Even fixtures rated at the same Kelvin can vary between manufacturers. Source from the same family when possible. When mixing is intentional, make the difference substantial (1500K+ apart). When uniform, stay within 300K range.

SCENE 3Building Your Fixture Schedule5:00–7:00
[VISUAL: Spreadsheet template]

Your master document: Type Code, Description, Brand/Model, Watts, Lumens, CCT, CRI, Beam Angle, IP/IK, Finish, Quantity, Location, Driver, Dimming protocol, Notes. Contractors use it to source. Electricians use it for wiring. Clients use it for budget.

SCENE 4Final Tips7:00–7:30
[VISUAL: Quick-fire tips]

Always request physical samples. Test in actual space. Check appearance when turned off. Verify lead times. Budget 5–10% spares.
