Module 6

Documentation — Drawing & Presenting Light

Duration: 60 minutes  |  Objective: Communicate your lighting design through drawings, diagrams, and professional presentations.

Lesson 6.1 — The Art of Drawing Light (25 mins)

Light Drawing Representation Guide

ElementHow to Represent
Beam (downward)Triangular cone from fixture to surface
Beam (wide)Broader triangle, fading at edges
Wall washingGradient from top to bottom on wall
Indirect (upward)Cone from fixture upward, diffusing on ceiling
LED strip/coveLinear gradient along architectural edge
OmnidirectionalRadiating circles or soft glow
DecorativeSmall star or circle with radiating lines
Natural lightWide, soft shading from window/skylight
💡 Remember: You are drawing the light, not the objects. Furniture and walls are context. Light beams and glows are your actual design.

Lesson 6.2 — From Sketches to Plans (20 mins)

Three Essential Drawing Types

  1. Plan View (Top Down) — fixture locations, beam projections as circles, composition
  2. Section/Elevation (Side View) — vertical distribution, beam directions, installation heights
  3. Perspective (3D View) — atmosphere, color, how the space actually feels
