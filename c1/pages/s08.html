SCENE 1Opening0:00–0:30
[VISUAL: Beautifully lit interiors]

Now we learn to compose light — arranging elements for rhythm, balance, drama, and coherence.

SCENE 2What Is Composition?0:30–2:30
[VISUAL: Well-composed vs. poorly composed spaces]

Like photography or music, it's arranging elements into a unified whole. But light is invisible until it hits something. The space between light and shadow is where composition lives. Too much = flat. Too little = confusing. The magic is in the balance.

SCENE 3Principles2:30–5:30
[VISUAL: Animated diagrams of each principle]

Scale & Proportion — match fixtures to space size. Rhythm & Repetition — repeating elements guide the eye. Contrast — your most powerful tool; light needs shadow. Balance — doesn't mean symmetry; Madre Café was all about asymmetry. Direction — top-down feels natural; up-lighting dramatic; side-lighting reveals texture.

SCENE 4The Black Box Approach5:30–7:30
[VISUAL: Black room with lights added one by one]

Start with complete darkness. Add your first element — natural light from a window. Then a pendant. Then an LED strip. Always asking: does this support my concept? This subtractive thinking — building from darkness — is the opposite of most people's approach.

SCENE 5Madre Café Composition7:30–9:30
[VISUAL: Floor plans with lighting annotations]

First: natural light from skylight and pivoting door. Second: the light arch (primary detail). Third: downlighting on tables. Fourth: perimeter wall-washing. Four layers, each with clear function and hierarchy. The arch draws the eye first.
