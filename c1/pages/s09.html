If composition is the art, layers and hierarchies are the engineering. Two of the most practical tools in your toolkit.

SCENE 2What Are Layers?0:30–2:00
[VISUAL: Separate layers stacked]

Layers are independent groups of light, each controllable separately. Like tracks in music. Ambient, Task, Accent, Decorative, Natural. Not every space needs five — a bedroom might need three, a museum six.

SCENE 3What Are Hierarchies?2:00–3:30
[VISUAL: Same space with different lights emphasized]

Hierarchy determines where the eye goes first. Established through contrast. A candle in a dark room has enormous hierarchy. The same candle in sunlight has zero. Context creates hierarchy.

SCENE 4Madre Café Layers3:30–5:00
[VISUAL: Color-coded layer diagram]

Layer 1: Light arch (primary). Layer 2: Table downlights (secondary). Layer 3: Wall washing (tertiary). Layer 4: Skylight (dynamic natural). During bright afternoon, Layers 1–3 at 30%. After sunset, 100%.

SCENE 5Assignment & Transition5:00–6:00
[VISUAL: Assignment checklist]

Define your layers (minimum 3). Identify primary, secondary, tertiary. Document as a color-coded diagram. Next unit: technique — fixtures, light sources, and practical tools.
