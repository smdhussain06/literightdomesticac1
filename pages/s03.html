SCENE 1Course Roadmap0:00–1:00
[VISUAL: Animated roadmap showing 3 pillars]

Taking this course brings you closer to the culture of light. First, you'll learn what light is and how we perceive it, along with basic architectural lighting techniques. I'll share how we solve our projects using three parts: conceptualization, technique, and documentation.

SCENE 2The Three Parts1:00–2:00
[VISUAL: Animated icons for each part]

In conceptualization, you'll define your lighting project — your core idea, set of rules, and the sensation you want to provoke. You'll observe material properties and use them for your design.

In technique, you'll learn what a luminaire is, how to select one, and sometimes how to create your own custom fixtures.

In documentation, you'll learn to draw luminaires in plan and section, explain hierarchies and layers to clients, and graphically communicate your core idea.

SCENE 3Case Study & Final Project2:00–3:00
[VISUAL: Madre Café images, floor plans]

Throughout this course, I'll discuss the Madre Café project — situated in a mansion from the Mexican Porfiriato era. For your final project, you'll enhance a space layout with your lighting concepts, following all three steps.

One important principle: we always start by conceptualizing our spaces as a black box, to which we subsequently add light. Now join me in the next unit where I'll explain what light is.
