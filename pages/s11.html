Knowing technologies is half the battle. The real skill is selection — matching the right fixture to the right application.

SCENE 2The Selection Framework0:30–3:00
[VISUAL: Flowchart animation]

Step 1: Start with your concept. Step 2: Identify the function (ambient/focal/brilliants). Step 3: Define practical requirements (location, IP, voltage). Step 4: Set performance targets (CCT, CRI, beam angle, lumens). Step 5: Consider aesthetics (visible or hidden?). Step 6: Evaluate budget (fixture + driver + installation + maintenance).

SCENE 3Fixture Categories3:00–6:30
[VISUAL: 3D room with fixtures installed]

Recessed — clean lines, needs ceiling cavity. Surface-mounted — when recessing isn't possible. Suspended — double-height spaces, decorative. Track-mounted — galleries, retail, flexible. Wall-mounted — texture and corridors. Floor/Table — task and personal. LED strips — ambient and accent workhorse.

SCENE 4Professional Description6:30–10:00
[VISUAL: Template walkthrough]

Example: Tecnolite recessed panel — 10W, 970lm, 97 lm/W, 3000K, CRI 82, 120°, 25,000hrs, IP30, IK4, ABS housing, 125mm cut, 0.42kg. Example LED strip: SIECLED — 2700K, CRI 95, 730lm/m, 7.5W/m, 115°, cuts every 2.1cm.

SCENE 5Design Questions10:00–12:30
[VISUAL: Checklist appearing]

Does it do its job? Is it appropriate for the activities? Is the lamp exposed or concealed? How does it emit light — omni or directional? Does its aesthetic match your concept?
