SCENE 1Welcome0:00–0:30
[VISUAL: Avatar in warmly lit studio. Title card]

Welcome to "Lighting Design for Interior Spaces." I'm thrilled to have you here. In this course, you'll learn to see light not as something you take for granted, but as a powerful design material — one that can transform any space from ordinary to extraordinary.

I'm a lighting designer and the founder of a lighting design studio. At my studio, we create spaces of light. We design lighting for restaurants, offices, residences, galleries, and public buildings. But more than that, we craft experiences. Because every space tells a story, and light is the narrator.

SCENE 2My Journey into Lighting0:30–3:00
[VISUAL: Architectural spaces, universities, design studios]

Let me share how I got here. I studied architecture, but during my studies, I discovered that light was what truly fascinated me. I realized that a beautiful building without thoughtful lighting is only half-finished. Light gives architecture its voice.

After graduating, I started exploring lighting professionally. In Mexico, the field of lighting design wasn't as established as in Europe or Japan — there weren't many dedicated lighting design firms. So I had to carve my own path.

I began writing about light. One of my earliest works focused on light pollution in Mexico City — a topic very few were addressing at the time. That paper actually won me a prize: an opportunity to present at a conference in Germany.

SCENE 3The World of Light Opens Up3:00–5:30
[VISUAL: Conference halls, lighting exhibitions, scientific diagrams]

That trip to Germany was a turning point. At the conference, I discovered the incredible breadth of the lighting world. It's not just about architecture — it involves scientists studying the human eye and how we perceive light. There are experts in perception, psychology, luminaire development, laser technology. There are so many ways to study and work with light.

That's when I knew: this was my world. I was going to dedicate my career to lighting design. When I returned, I founded my studio with a clear mission: to bring the culture of light to every project we touched.

SCENE 4Self-Managed Projects5:30–9:00
[VISUAL: Gallery spaces, light installations, sun paths]

One of the most important things I've learned is the power of self-managed projects. I created a project called "There's No Place Like Home" — an on-site intervention in an empty architectural space, essentially a bare gray shell.

My goal was to channel natural light into this space and explore how sunlight interacted with the environment. We built structures specifically to trap and redirect sunlight. We painted surfaces in specific colors that altered how the interior perceived sunlight. We also added one artificial light source to create contrast and observe the difference between static artificial light and the ever-moving natural light.

This was a self-funded project. Part of the budget came from my own pocket, part from a sponsoring mentor who saw the intention behind the work, and part from the gallery. The investment paid off in ways money can't measure.

SCENE 5Documentation & Awards9:00–12:00
[VISUAL: LAMP Awards ceremony, European cities, project photos]

When we finished the project, we documented everything. Documentation is crucial. We took photographs, invited guests, organized tours. During some tours, I explained how sunlight moved through the space — because most people see sunlight every day but never stop to understand what's happening.

I submitted this project to the LAMP Awards, one of the most prestigious lighting design competitions in the world. And we became finalists. When I arrived in Spain, I discovered my competition wasn't just individual designers — these were entire companies with 50, even 200 employees. We were the only Latin Americans among the finalists.

We didn't win the grand prize. But the experience taught me that the intention of the light is what truly matters, not the size of your company or budget.

SCENE 6What We Do & Transition12:00–14:00
[VISUAL: Studio portfolio — restaurants, offices, public spaces]

If you want to achieve success in this field, you must invest in your work. Document everything. Enter competitions. Push boundaries. No one is going to demand this work from you except yourself.

At my studio, I have the freedom to do everything I enjoy: lighting design, crafting spaces with light, and creating custom luminaires. The journey hasn't always been straightforward — at the beginning, the path wasn't clear. But now I am very happy with what we do.

Now that you know who I am and what I'm dedicated to, join me in the next lesson where I'll talk about my influences — the designers and artists who have shaped my approach to light.
